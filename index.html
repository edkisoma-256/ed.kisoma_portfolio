About

One creative, several crafts

Edward is a Visual Storytelling and Strategic Communications Professional who helps organisations transform complex ideas into clear, engaging, and impactful communication. With at least 10 years of experience across public health, government, nonprofit, and international organisations, he leads multidisciplinary creative projects that combine strategy, design, storytelling, and technology to educate audiences, strengthen brands, and communicate organisational impact. His expertise spans graphic design, video production, documentary filmmaking, photography, editorial design, motion graphics, illustration, digital communications, and live broadcast production.

Currently serving as Video Administrator (Creative Production Lead) at Worship Harvest Ministries he oversees multimedia production supporting an international movement across four continents while mentoring a multidisciplinary creative team. Edward is recognised for combining strategic thinking with hands-on execution to deliver communication that is visually compelling, audience-centred, and results-driven

He holds a Bachelor's Degree in Industrial and Fine Arts from the Margaret Trowell School of Industrial and Fine Arts, Makerere University. His faith shapes much of what he makes — from church production work to storytelling that treats its audience with care.

SCENE 01 CLIENT MUJHU / Young Generation Alive TYPE illustration
ConceptIllustrationHealth Comms

the DTG Explainer

The Brief

Young people living with HIV needed health information that spoke to their real questions and fears — concerning the new drug that they'd heard about.

The Approach

Created a scene at a health center with a doctor and young people, where they expressed their fears and myths around the drug.

What It Delivered

A visual format the health team could return to for new topics as they came up, and a non-technical way for frontline workers to explain a fast-moving safety update to patients.

SCENE 02 CLIENT Makerere University Joint AIDS Program (MUJHU) TYPE Illustrant & Animation Storyboard
StoryboardIllustration & animation

Public Health Communication

The Brief

MUJHU needed to communicate dense public-health information about EMTCT (Eliminate Mother-to-Child Transmission of HIV), Adherence and Viral Load and HIV Cure myths to ordinary people (likely young mothers, pregnant women, or youth audiences)

The Approach

Created Jojo, a peer educator young people would relate with and used her to explain the different topics in different scenes.

What It Delivered

Adressed myths and fears young people had and helped them adhere to their medication hence improving their health
